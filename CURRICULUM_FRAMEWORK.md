# Bunny Dash — Curriculum & Learning Framework

## Core Philosophy

The learning in Bunny Dash is **never corrective and always additive.** Every question a child encounters assumes they are capable of the answer with the right support. The system builds knowledge cumulatively — each session layering on what came before, never tearing down what was learned.

For children in medical treatment, learning continuity is about **bridging the gap**, not filling a deficit. The language, pacing, and feedback in every learning interaction reflects this.

## Reading & Language Arts Curriculum Map

### How Reading Works in Bunny Dash

Reading checkpoints use a modified quiz screen:
1. A short passage appears (narrated aloud automatically)
2. 1–2 comprehension questions follow
3. Tap-to-select answer tiles (4 options) replace the numeric keypad
4. Audio replay button always visible — children can re-hear the passage

### Passage Themes
All passages are:
- Animals, nature, adventure, friendship — nothing medical or illness-related in the content
- Culturally diverse (rotate characters from different world cultures)
- Positively framed — stories of curiosity, discovery, persistence
- Grade-appropriate Lexile levels

### Grade K–1
- 2–3 sentences, large text, image support
- Topics: colors, shapes, animals, numbers in stories
- Questions: What color is the bunny? / How many apples were there?

### Grade 2–3
- 1 short paragraph (4–6 sentences)
- Topics: simple narratives, factual texts about animals/nature
- Questions: What happened first? / What is the main idea?

### Grade 4–5
- 2 paragraphs
- Topics: biographies of explorers and inventors (age-appropriate), nature documentaries
- Questions: What does the word "___" mean here? / What evidence supports this?

### Grade 6–8
- 3–4 paragraphs; mixed fiction and non-fiction
- Topics: historical events, science discoveries, literary excerpts from classic/contemporary works
- Questions: Author's purpose, inference, compare/contrast, context clues

### Vocabulary Builder (Embedded)
After any passage, 1–2 highlighted words appear with:
- Tap-to-hear pronunciation
- Tap-to-see definition (child-friendly)
- The word is added to the player's personal "Star Word" list (viewable in profile)
- Star Words reappear in later questions to reinforce retention

### Creative Writing Corner (Optional)
Available from the home screen, not a level checkpoint:
- A writing prompt chosen by the player's bunny (bunny "asks" a question)
- Player types or speaks their answer (voice recording)
- Saved to their portfolio — viewable by parent/teacher
- Examples:
  - "Fluff wants to know: What is the most amazing thing you've seen today?"
  - "If you could visit any world in Bunny Realm, what would you do there?"

## Science Curriculum Map

### Integration with Game Worlds

Science questions are thematically linked to the current game world — this reinforces learning through context.

| World | Theme | Science Topics |
|---|---|---|
| Meadow Fields | Plants, insects | Life cycles, photosynthesis basics, insect anatomy |
| Crystal Caves | Minerals, light | Light reflection/refraction, rock types, earth layers |
| Cloud Kingdom | Weather, atmosphere | Water cycle, clouds, weather patterns, air pressure |
| Rainbow Realm | Light spectrum, optics | ROY G BIV, prisms, why sky is blue |
| Star Harbor | Space | Planets, solar system, gravity, stars |
| Sunlit Summit | Energy, physics | Potential/kinetic energy, simple machines, temperature |

### Question Format Variety

**Observation Questions:** "The bunny is standing in sunlight. Which direction does the shadow point?" → Tap direction on diagram

**Sorting/Classification:** Drag-and-drop tiles (4 items) into two categories: "Living" or "Non-living"

**Fill-in:** "Water changes from liquid to gas through a process called ___." → Tap the correct word tile

**Numeric:** "A spider has ___ legs." → Keypad (8)

**Sequence:** Place 4 steps of the water cycle in order (tap to reorder)

## Adaptive Learning Engine — Detailed Spec

### Performance Window
The engine evaluates the last **10 questions answered per subject** to determine difficulty direction:

```
Score ≥ 8/10 correct  →  Step UP difficulty (+0.5 level)
Score 5–7/10 correct  →  HOLD current difficulty
Score ≤ 4/10 correct  →  Step DOWN difficulty (−0.5 level)
```

Difficulty is a floating-point value per subject (not tied to game level), allowing fine-grained positioning within a grade's content.

### Hint System
Available on every question via a "?" button:
- **Level 1 hint:** Narrated tip from the bunny ("Think about groups of things!")
- **Level 2 hint:** Shows a worked example of the same type (different numbers)
- **Level 3 hint:** Shows the answer calculation step-by-step
Each hint costs 2 Star Coins (encouraging judicious use without punishing those who need help)

### "Try This Way" Bridge
When a player gets the same topic wrong 3 times in sequence, the system doesn't just lower difficulty — it switches **teaching approach**:
- Math: Switches from symbolic (3 × 4) to visual (3 groups of 4 shown as dots) to worded (three baskets, each with four apples)
- Reading: Switches from text extraction to inference question
The bridge style is logged so future sessions start with the approach that worked.

## Catch-Up Plan System (Teacher Feature)

### How It Works

A teacher provides:
1. Current class's position in the curriculum (chapter/unit/standard codes)
2. The absent student's last known position before illness onset

The system generates a **personalized catch-up curriculum queue**:
- Topics the class covered while the student was absent
- Ordered by prerequisite dependency (can't learn long division without knowing basic division)
- Estimated completion time per topic
- Injected into the student's quiz rotation as priority questions

### Output Example

```
Student: [Player Name]
Absent since: October 3
Class is currently at: CCSS.MATH.4.NBT.B.5 (Multiply 2-digit × 2-digit)

Catch-up plan generated:
  1. CCSS.MATH.4.OA.A.1   — Multiplicative comparisons     (est. 3 sessions)
  2. CCSS.MATH.4.OA.A.2   — Multiplication word problems   (est. 4 sessions)
  3. CCSS.MATH.4.NBT.B.4  — Multi-digit addition/subtraction (est. 2 sessions)
  4. CCSS.MATH.4.NBT.B.5  — 2-digit × 2-digit multiplication (est. 6 sessions)

Estimated full catch-up: ~15 sessions (~3 weeks at 5 sessions/week)
```

This plan is visible to both the teacher and parent, updating dynamically as the student makes progress.

## Content Review & Quality Standards

All curriculum content in Bunny Dash is:
1. **Educator-reviewed:** Each question reviewed by a certified teacher in the relevant grade/subject
2. **Medically appropriate:** All content reviewed by a child life specialist to ensure nothing triggers distress for children in treatment
3. **Culturally diverse:** Representation across gender, ethnicity, ability in all characters and passages
4. **Non-violent:** Zero violent content; conflict resolution always modeled positively
5. **Regularly updated:** Curriculum packs versioned and updated annually to reflect standard revisions

---

*The curriculum framework exists to serve one purpose: when a child finishes treatment and walks back into their classroom, they should feel ready — not left behind. Every question, every hint, every catch-up plan works toward that single goal.*
